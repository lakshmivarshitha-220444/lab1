Introduction

The city public transport system is designed to provide efficient and reliable transportation options for residents and visitors.
With a variety of routes and services, the system aims to connect different parts of the city seamlessly.

The common modes of public transport include buses, trains, airlines, and trams. Each mode has its own set of routes that cater to different areas and travel needs.

Overview of some basic public transport modes:

Bus Routes

Bus routes cover extensive areas of the city, providing frequent stops and connections to major landmarks and neighborhoods.

Buses are a cost-effective way to navigate the city.

pros:

  • Affordable fares
  • Extensive coverage
  • Frequent service

cons:

  • Can be crowded during peak hours
  • Subject to traffic delays

Train Routes

Train routes provide faster travel options for longer distances within the city and to suburban areas.

Trains are known for their punctuality and comfort.

pros:

  • Fast and efficient
  • Comfortable seating
  • Less affected by traffic

cons:

  • Limited coverage in some areas
  • Higher fares compared to buses
